About Maiya

A classmate focused on making SOM feel stronger, more welcoming, and more student-centered.

Maiya Cook is running to build a student government that feels present, clear, and grounded in the day-to-day realities of SOM life.

This campaign is rooted in the belief that students should feel seen, supported, and proud of the community they are shaping together.

What this campaign stands for

  • Thoughtful leadership with visible action
  • Community-building that feels intentional
  • Advocacy that listens and follows through

Why I’m Running

Because the best parts of SOM happen when people feel connected to each other.

This campaign starts from a simple belief: community is not a side benefit of SOM. It is one of the reasons this place matters so much.

Maiya is running to help protect that feeling, strengthen it, and make sure student leadership reflects the care, energy, and collaboration already present in the class.

Three Priorities

A clear platform built around community, transparency, and support.

01

Stronger student community

Create more opportunities for students to connect across programs, interests, and shared experiences so SOM feels more cohesive and energizing.

02

Transparent leadership

Make communication clearer so students know what is being heard, what is being worked on, and how student government is moving priorities forward.

03

Student support and advocacy

Lead with empathy and accountability by advocating for student needs across academics, campus life, and the everyday SOM experience.

Building Community

Creating the kinds of moments that make classmates feel like a real community.

Strong communities grow through shared experiences. Maiya wants to support more moments that bring students together across programs, interests, and friend groups.

That means a student experience that feels more welcoming, more energizing, and more intentionally connected throughout the year.

Leadership Through Action

Turning care for the community into visible work and consistent follow-through.

Leadership matters most when it is tangible. Maiya’s approach is grounded in showing up, listening closely, and doing the work needed to make student concerns move forward.

The goal is not just to speak for students, but to build trust through action that is clear, accountable, and community-minded.
